Reflection on Creating a Website for 30 MFF (26 Feb 2019)

Project Description: what is the project, what is the overall concept and theme, and what kind of experience you were striving to create.

I created a website for the Coffee Mixer movie that Wani, Keith and I created in the Communications Lab class. The movie was about a guy who mistakes someone else’s coffee cup for his and gets into an argument about it. The barista comes in with his original cup of coffee to resolve the argument. I made the theme a desk where someone might be drinking coffee and working. I wanted to put the user in the character’s point of view. I wanted to create a simple experience about being at a desk drinking coffee and working that feels straightforward. There would be items on the desk like a coffee cup, a laptop and a pair of headphones that the user could click on to listen to music from the film and meet the creators.

Process: How did you go about implementing the concept.

At first my wireframe was a more like a traditional website, but I then I had this idea of making the background a desk. So I went to Blacksmith and filmed a top-down view of a desk with a cup of coffee, working papers, a phone and pair of headphones it at each corner. Then I had this idea that to transition to another page the objects could move out of the frame, so I asked Steven to help me move the items out of the frame in a short video. I wrote up the html code and made the desk the background. I placed text over each object: 'read' over the papers, 'meet' over the coffee mug, 'listen' over the headphones, 'watch' over the phone. I placed the title of the movie in the center of the page. The user can click on each piece of text and navigate to a different page. For the 'read' page a I put in some text explaining the movie. For the 'meet' page, I put in bios of the creators in flex boxes. For the 'listen' page, I put embedded a spotify playlist with the iframe tag. For the 'watch' page, I embedded a youtube iframe of the movie. To make the transition where the objects move out of the frame, I made the background image hide to expose the animated gif of the objects moving out of the frame that I shot earlier. I set a 400 millisecond timeout for the computer to wait until the gif played to navigate to the page the user clicked on. I did this all with jquery (God Bless jquery!!!).

Reflection/Evaluation: This should bookend the initial concept and talk about whether your expectations and goals were met in the process of building the final working version.

My expectations were all met when I looked at the site locally. When I uploaded the site online, there was a huge lag that delayed the transition. I wish I had more time to fix this, but I’m not sure how I would even start.
